What this page is

This is the plain-English walkthrough for one algo page.

Biscotti is the example because it is the easiest one to read without fake cleverness.

The goal is simple: explain every box, chart, and table without jargon.

Top of the page

Big title = the algo name.

The line under it tells you when the lab started, when it last ran, how much money the sim starts with, and how much each trade uses.

The short sentence below that says what the signal is trying to do.

The stats boxes

Total equity = what the sim is worth now.

Net P&L = dollars up or down from the starting amount.

Return = the same thing, but in percent.

Win rate = percent of closed trades that made money.

Sharpe ratio = return versus wobble; useful, but not magic.

Max drawdown = worst drop from a prior peak.

Avg win / Avg loss = the average size of winning and losing trades.

Realized / Unrealized = locked-in profit versus open profit or loss.

The chart

Portfolio Equity = the algo line.

SPY Benchmark = the market yardstick.

Starting Capital = the flat line where the sim began.

Above SPY is better than the benchmark. Below SPY is not.

The open position box

This shows what the algo holds right now.

Ticker = what it owns.

Entry = what it paid.

Current = the latest price.

Unrealized = gain or loss that is still floating.

Value = dollar size of the position.

Opened = when the trade started.

The trade log

This is the paper-trade history.

Date = when the trade happened.

Action = buy or sell.

Price and shares = trade size.

P&L = what the trade made or lost.

Notes = why the trade happened, usually rebalance or CASH.

What the colors mean

Green usually means positive or active.

Red usually means negative or losing.

Muted gray means nothing special to call out.

The colors help you read fast. They are not there to hide anything.

How to read the whole page

Start at the top.

Read the numbers.

Check the chart.

Then read the open position and trade log.

If the page looks ugly, that is usually the point.
